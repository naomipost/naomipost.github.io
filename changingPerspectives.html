Reflecting on My First Semester

Changing Perspectives

My perspective on the Health and Well-Being pillar changed quite a bit over the semester. I always knew that there were at least two dimensions of well-being, physical and mental, but I had no idea that there were eight more dimensions! This was a big shock to me, and made me realize that there are so many more important parts of my life worth taking care of.

One of the most interesting dimensions of well-being that I learned this semester was environmental well-being. I never really thought about how the environment can influence your well-being, but it definitely is an important aspect. I never feel great after staying inside all day; even going on a walk to eat at Abel can lift my mood a bit.

I didn't focus on all 10 dimensions of well-being this semester, but two that I tried to focus on were physical and emotional well-being. Since I'm not doing cross country anymore like I did in high school, I still wanted to make sure I was getting exercise and eating healthy. To get exercise, I joined the judo club, and I try my best to eat healthy, but sometimes the only good thing on the menu is chicken strips. A few weeks ago my roommate and I cooked some steaks, which made me excited for next year when I have an apartment. To improve my emotional well-being, my roommate and I visited the Nebraska Humane Society. Neither of us can have a cat at the moment, but it was really fun to go and play with the cats there.
